EINC Protocol

Is a package of evidence-based practices for newborncare constitute a series of time-bound, chronologically-ordered, standard procedures that birthing mothers and their newborns receive during labor, delivery, and at birth respectively.

Four Time-Bound Interventions to the Newborn

1. Immediate and Thorough Drying at Delivery

Immediate and thorough drying of the newborn prevents hypothermia which is extremely important to newborn survival.

Source:https://www.brooksidepress.org/Products/Military_OBGYN/Textbook/Newborn/BabyDry.jpg

2. Skin-to-Skin Contact of Mother and Baby

Keeping the mother and baby in an uninterrupted skin-to-skin contact prevents hypothermia, hypoglycemia, and sepsis, increses colonization with protective bacterial flora and improved breastfeeding initiation and exclusivity.

Source:https://images.app.goo.gl/cpjYge5yCLVddacR8

3. Properly Timed Cord Clamping within 1-3 Minutes of Birth or when Cord Pulsation Stops

Properly timed cord clamping and cutting until the umbilical cord pulsation stops decreases anemia in one out od seven term babies and one out of every three preterm babies. It also prevents brain (intraventricular) hemorrhage in one of two preterm babies.

Source:What is delayed cord clamping and should I do it? | BabyCentre

4. Non-Separation of Mother and Baby to Encourage Early Breastfeeding Initiation

Breastfeeding initiation withing the first hour of life prevents an estimated 19.1% of all neonatal deaths.
